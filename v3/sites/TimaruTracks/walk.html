Centennial Park, commonly known as “The Scenic” by locals is a large forest area that used to incorporate many limestone quarries but now has been repurposed to incorporate a myriad of walking and cycling tracks, Centennial Park also includes a small pond, playgrounds, free-to-use BBQs and picnic area.

Lookout Areas

Dashing Rocks walkway follows the coast north of Timaru township, it offers views of Timaru, Caroline Bay, and out to sea. Waimataitai beach is a rock and clay beach that the walkway skirts around, if you turn off onto Waimataitai beach you can find multitudes of wildlife in rock pools. At the Washdyke Lagoon end of the walkway you can find multitudes of seabirds and an information sign about the shrinking of the Washdyke Lagoon.

Caroline Bay is a sand beach formed when the port was built, most of it is reclaimed land. The dunes and subsequent boardwalk was developed in 2008. Throughout parts of December and January there is a carnival held at Caroline Bay and there are other events held throughout the year at Caroline Bay. To find out more about events being held at Caroline Bay visit the Caroline Bay Association’s Website.

Saltwater Creek

Swimming is Prohibited
Birdlife
Sealife
This path may be dangerous in winter.

Saltwater Creek and the adjoining Otipua Wetlands is a birdlife and waterlife rich area. Northen Saltwater Creek track runs beside Saltwater Creek from Fairview Rd to State Highway one or vice-versa. Southern Saltwater Creek Track allows access to Patiti Point, Jack's Point, and Otipua Wetlands. The Otipua Wetlands have huge amounts of birds and is a vital area for birds in the Timaru area.
